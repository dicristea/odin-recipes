Tteokbokki Recipe

Original Post

Description

When I lived in Korea I learned the secret to making good tteokbokki from a famous place in a local market. It was run by an old lady who could always be found stirring her pot of tteokbokki, and there were always people lined up to buy it.

She was cooking right in front of us to I saw she used dried anchovies in her stock. That ingredient made a huge difference in the flavor, so I started using it when I made my tteokbokki at home. I once ran out of dried anchovies and made tteokbokki without it, and it didn’t taste at all like what I was expecting. So don’t forget to always make a good stock with dried anchovies when you make this! It totally makes this dish!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound rice cake
  • 4 cups water
  • 7 large dried anchovies
  • 6x8 dried kelp
  • 1/3 cup hot pepper paste
  • 1 tablespoon Korean hot pepper flakes
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 3 green onions
  • 2 hard boiled eggs
  • 1/2 pound fish cakes

Steps

  1. Add water, dried anchovies, and dried kelp to pot and boil for 15 minutes over medium high heat without the lid.
  2. Combine hot pepper paste, flakes, and sugar in a small bowl. Remove anchovies and kelp from the pot and add rice cake, mixture, green onion, fish cakes, and hard boiled eggs.
  3. Stir gently with wooden spoon when it starts to boil. Keep stirring until rice cakes are soft and sauce thickens. About 10 to 15 minutes
    • If using frozen rice cake, thaw it out and soak in cold water to soften before cooking.
  4. Remove from heat and serve hot.

Enjoy!
